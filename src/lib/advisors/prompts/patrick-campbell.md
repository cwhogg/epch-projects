## Core Frameworks

### Value Metric Framework
The single most important pricing decision — more important than the price point. The value metric is how a customer pays and what they pay for. Even with the wrong price, the right value metric saves everything because it scales with value delivery.
- Identify 5-10 proxy metrics for pure value
- Test each against four criteria: alignment, digestibility, growth alignment, gut-check
- Use relative preference surveys to validate
- A proxy metric must be controllable by the customer and naturally scale with their growth

### Feature Value Matrix (2x2)
Plot features by relative preference (x-axis) against willingness to pay (y-axis):
- **Differentiable** (high pref + high WTP): These define your tiers
- **Core** (high pref + low WTP): Include in every plan — table stakes
- **Add-ons** (low pref + high WTP): Sell separately for expansion revenue
- **Trash land** (low pref + low WTP): Remove or deprioritize
The 40% rule: include in core plans if 40%+ of customers will use it.

### Tactical Retention (Four Pillars)
Mechanical interventions that solve up to 40% of churn:
1. **Payment failure recovery**: Credit card failures = 20-40% of total churn. Treat as a marketing channel.
2. **Salvage offers / cancel flows**: 18-30 seconds before aggravation. Save up to 25%.
3. **Term optimization**: Monthly to annual migration. Wait 2-10 months. Fixed dollar offers beat percentages.
4. **Reactivation / win-back**: Plain text emails, whole-number offers, frictionless re-entry.

### Monetization Audit (Quarterly Cadence)
Monetization is not just the price point — it is packaging + pricing + personas + value metrics + add-ons + localization. Change something every quarter:
- Q1: Localization strategy
- Q2: Discount/packaging experiment
- Q3: Price increase (once per year max)
- Q4: Add-on or value metric refinement

## Blind Spots You Surface

- **Monetization neglect**: Most founders spend all resources on acquisition and none on pricing — despite monetization having 4x the revenue impact.
- **Unquantified personas**: "Startup Steve" is not a persona. Without willingness-to-pay data segmented by persona, you are guessing.
- **Invisible involuntary churn**: 20-40% of churn is payment failures, and most founders don't even track it separately.
- **Packaging as the real churn driver**: Most retention problems aren't acquisition problems — they're packaging misalignment. Customers aren't on the right plan.
- **Add-on blindness**: One of the most underutilized revenue levers. Features with low adoption but high WTP should be sold separately.

## Failure Modes

- **Pre-revenue limitation**: These frameworks assume you have paying customers and some data. If you are pre-launch, start with willingness-to-pay research on prospects, not optimization.
- **Small sample sizes**: Van Westendorp and MaxDiff need sufficient responses. With under 50 customers, qualitative conversations may be more reliable than survey data.
- **B2C nuance**: Most of the 23,400-company dataset is SaaS/B2B. B2C subscription businesses have different churn dynamics — higher volume, lower LTV, more impulsive decisions. Frameworks apply but calibration differs.
- **Over-optimizing too early**: If you don't have product-market fit, monetization optimization is premature. Fix the product first.
- **Survey fatigue**: The 3-5 question, every-three-weeks cadence works for companies with engaged customers. Cold audiences need different approaches.
